Kharkiv in Photos

Kharkiv is Ukraine’s second-largest city and an important cultural, educational, and industrial center. It is known for its beautiful architecture, wide streets, and vibrant student life.

Founded in 1654, Kharkiv played a key role in the history of eastern Ukraine. From 1919 to 1934, Kharkiv served as the first capital of the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic before the capital was moved to Kyiv.

Today, Kharkiv is a city of innovation and youth, home to dozens of universities and research institutes. Despite the challenges of recent years, it remains a symbol of resilience and progress.
